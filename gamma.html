Let's explore Gamma Decay !!

In this page, we will explore another nuclear process called Gamma Decay.

Gamma decay occurs when an unstable nucleus has excess energy. Instead of releasing particles like alpha or beta decay, the nucleus releases this extra energy in the form of gamma radiation.

Gamma radiation is a type of high-energy electromagnetic wave. This radiation allows the nucleus to move from a high-energy state to a lower-energy and more stable state.

You should notice that during gamma decay, no particles are lost from the nucleus. Instead, the nucleus simply releases energy as gamma radiation. This means the number of protons and neutrons stays the same.

Key Notes:

  • Gamma radiation is a high-energy electromagnetic wave.
  • No particles are emitted during gamma decay.
  • The atomic number and mass number stay the same.
  • Gamma radiation is very penetrating and requires thick lead or concrete to stop it.
