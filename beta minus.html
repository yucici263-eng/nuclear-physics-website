In this page, we will explore Beta Minus Decay (β⁻ decay).

Beta minus decay happens when an unstable nucleus has too many neutrons. To stabilise itself, a neutron converts into a proton. During this process, an electron (beta particle) is emitted.

The emitted electron travels away from the nucleus at high speed, carrying energy away from the nucleus.

This is how beta minus decay happens

During beta minus decay, a neutron becomes a proton. This increases the atomic number of the element by one, helping the nucleus move toward stability.

Key Notes:

  • A beta particle is a fast-moving electron.
  • Beta minus decay occurs when a neutron converts into a proton.
  • The atomic number increases by 1.
  • Beta radiation can travel further than alpha radiation.
